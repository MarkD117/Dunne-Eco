Our History

A Legacy of Innovation and Resilience: Honoring over a century of craftsmanship, from our humble beginnings in 1903 to our commitment to excellence today.

The Dunne Legacy

Dunnes Workshop was founded in 1903 by Charles Dunne, a remarkably innovative craftsman who powered his sawmill with a steam engine. Known as both a skilled journeyman and a wheelwright, Charles crafted wheels and carts for local farmers. After his tragic passing in 1942, his wife Annie and son Joe took on the challenge of running the business, with support from Charles Harte. Together, they upheld the Dunne tradition through difficult times.

A new era began in 1959 when Joe married Anne. Facing the hardships of the 1960s, they worked tirelessly to grow the business, eventually building a team of over 40 employees by the 1980s, when their children Paul, Norah, and Ian joined the family trade.

In 2014, following Joe's passing, the company evolved further. Ian now leads the manufacturing of stairs, internal door kits, and external windows and doors, with a deep commitment to energy efficiency. At Dunne Eco, we are passionate about producing high-performance, passive-standard windows and doors, while staying true to our heritage in crafting quality stairs and internal doors.

Ian Dunne: Managing Director

The Journey

A Detailed Timeline Showcasing the Historic Milestones, Generational Dedication, and Craftsmanship That Shaped the Journey to Dunne Eco.

1st Generation

1903 - 1916

Charles Dunne started working as a journeyman tradesman. In 1915 he bought Blakefield where the original Workshop was started.

Steam Power

1916 - 1942

In 1916 Charles purchased a Ransome portable steam engine and a rack bench where he started to mill timber to manufacure cart wheels, shafts & component parts for bog barrows and all other farm equipment.

2nd Generation

1942 - 1950

After the sad passing of Charles, his son Joe Dunne had to leave school and start his apprenticeship under the guidance of Charlie Harte. Joe was a very quick learner and like his father before him, was a very innovative man.

House Building

1950 - 1970

Joe started building houses and the workshop was used to make the joinery for those houses. He progressed into building schools, banks and commercial buildings.

Joinery

1970 - 1980

Joe's love for joinery saw him purchase many machines and the business started to grow exponentially.

3rd Generation

1980 - 2014

Joe decided to close down the building and concentrate on the joinery. He was later joined by the 3rd generation who brought the business from 40 people to over 100 in 2003.

Dunne Eco Opens

2014 - 2018

Joe sadly passes away in mid 2014 and Dunnes Workshop was closed. Ian decided to go it alone and started Dunne Eco in 2015.

New Beginnings

2018 - Present

Ian was joined by Joseph Egan, the head foreman out of Dunnes Workshop. Dunne Eco really took off from there with the purchases of new machines to produce the quality of joinery that we do today.
